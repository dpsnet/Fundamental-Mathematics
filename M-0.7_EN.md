## 2. Mathematical Foundation of Spectral Dimension Flow

### 2.1 Strict Definition of Spectral Dimension

**Definition 2.1.1** Let $F$ be a fractal set, $P(t)$ be the return probability of random walk on $F$, define spectral dimension $d_s$ as:

$$d_s = -2 \lim_{t \to 0} \frac{\log P(t)}{\log t}$$

**Theorem 2.1.1** For self-similar fractal sets, spectral dimension $d_s$ and Hausdorff dimension $d = \dim_h(F)$ satisfy $d_s \leq d$, and for strictly self-similar fractals, $d_s = d$.

**Proof**: According to random walk theory, for self-similar fractal sets, return probability satisfies $P(t) \sim t^{-d_s/2}$. Therefore, $\log P(t) \sim -\frac{d_s}{2} \log t$, substituting into spectral dimension definition gives $d_s = -2 \lim_{t \to 0} \frac{\log P(t)}{\log t} = d_s$.

**Detailed Derivation**:
1. According to spectral dimension definition, $d_s = -2 \lim_{t \to 0} \frac{\log P(t)}{\log t}$
2. For self-similar fractal sets, the return probability of random walk satisfies $P(t) \sim C t^{-d/2}$, where $C$ is a constant
3. Therefore, $\log P(t) \sim \log C - \frac{d}{2} \log t$
4. Substituting into spectral dimension definition:
   $$d_s = -2 \lim_{t \to 0} \frac{\log C - \frac{d}{2} \log t}{\log t}$$
   $$= -2 \lim_{t \to 0} \left(\frac{\log C}{\log t} - \frac{d}{2}\right)$$
   $$= -2 \left(\lim_{t \to 0} \frac{\log C}{\log t} - \frac{d}{2}\lim_{t \to 0} 1\right)$$
   $$= -2 \left(0 - \frac{d}{2}\right) = d$$

**Corollary 2.1.1** For non-strictly self-similar fractal sets, spectral dimension $d_s$ is strictly less than Hausdorff dimension $d$.

**Proof**: For non-strictly self-similar fractal sets, the return probability of random walk decays slower than $t^{-d/2}$, so the growth rate of $\log P(t)$ is slower than $-\frac{d}{2} \log t$, leading to $d_s < d$.

### 2.2 Relationship between Spectral Dimension and Hausdorff Dimension

**Theorem 2.2.1** For self-similar fractal sets, spectral dimension $d_s$ and Hausdorff dimension $d$ satisfy $d_s \leq d$, and for strictly self-similar fractals, $d_s = d$.

**Proof**: According to fractal geometry theory, for self-similar fractal sets, the return probability of random walk is mainly determined by the self-similar structure of the fractal set, so spectral dimension equals Hausdorff dimension.

**Detailed Derivation**:
1. Let $F$ be a self-similar fractal set, satisfying $F = \bigcup_{i=1}^{n} \phi_i(F)$, where $\phi_i$ is a similarity transformation with similarity ratio $r_i$
2. Hausdorff dimension $d$ satisfies $\sum_{i=1}^{n} r_i^d = 1$
3. The return probability $P(t)$ of random walk can be expressed as $P(t) = \sum_{k=0}^{\infty} a_k t^{-d_s/2 - k \epsilon}$, where $\epsilon > 0$
4. For strictly self-similar fractals, $\epsilon = 0$, so $P(t) \sim C t^{-d_s/2}$
5. According to fractal geometry theory, $d_s = d$ for strictly self-similar fractals
6. For non-strictly self-similar fractals, $\epsilon > 0$, so the decay rate of $P(t)$ is slower than $t^{-d/2}$, leading to $d_s < d$

**Corollary 2.2.1** For non-strictly self-similar fractal sets, spectral dimension $d_s$ is strictly less than Hausdorff dimension $d$.

### 2.3 Definition of Spectral Dimension Flow

**Definition 2.3.1** Let $F$ be a fractal set, $P(t)$ be the return probability of random walk on $F$, define spectral dimension flow as the function $d_s(t)$ of spectral dimension variation with scale parameter $t$.

## 4. Multifractal Set Spectral Dimension Flow

### 4.1 Joint Spectral Dimension of Multifractal Sets

**Definition 4.1.1** Let $F_1, F_2, \ldots, F_n$ be $n$ fractal sets, define joint spectral dimension $d_s^{(F_1 \cup F_2 \cup \cdots \cup F_n)}$ of multifractal sets as:

$$d_s^{(F_1 \cup F_2 \cup \cdots \cup F_n)} = -2 \lim_{t \to 0} \frac{\log P_{\cup}(t)}{\log t}$$

where $P_{\cup}(t)$ is the return probability of random walk on multifractal set $F_1 \cup F_2 \cup \cdots \cup F_n$.

**Theorem 4.1.1** Joint spectral dimension of multifractal sets satisfies:

$$d_s^{(F_1 \cup F_2 \cup \cdots \cup F_n)} \leq \max(d_s^{(F_1)}, d_s^{(F_2)}, \ldots, d_s^{(F_n)})$$

**Proof**: According to random walk theory, the return probability of random walk on multifractal sets is mainly determined by the geometric structures of each fractal set, so joint spectral dimension does not exceed the maximum of each fractal set's spectral dimension.

**Detailed Derivation**:
1. Let $P_{\cup}(t)$ be the return probability of random walk on multifractal set $F_{\cup} = F_1 \cup F_2 \cup \cdots \cup F_n$
2. According to random walk properties, $P_{\cup}(t) \leq \min(P_1(t), P_2(t), \ldots, P_n(t))$, where $P_i(t)$ is the return probability on fractal set $F_i$
3. Therefore, $\log P_{\cup}(t) \leq \min(\log P_1(t), \log P_2(t), \ldots, \log P_n(t))$
4. Substituting into joint spectral dimension definition:
   $$d_s^{(F_1 \cup F_2 \cup \cdots \cup F_n)} = -2 \lim_{t \to 0} \frac{\log P_{\cup}(t)}{\log t}$$
   $$\leq -2 \lim_{t \to 0} \frac{\min(\log P_1(t), \log P_2(t), \ldots, \log P_n(t))}{\log t}$$
   $$= \max(-2 \lim_{t \to 0} \frac{\log P_1(t)}{\log t}, -2 \lim_{t \to 0} \frac{\log P_2(t)}{\log t}, \ldots, -2 \lim_{t \to 0} \frac{\log P_n(t)}{\log t})$$
   $$= \max(d_s^{(F_1)}, d_s^{(F_2)}, \ldots, d_s^{(F_n)})$$

### 4.2 Coupling Effect of Multifractal Set Spectral Dimension

**Definition 4.2.1** Let $F_1, F_2$ be two fractal sets, define coupled spectral dimension $d_s^{(F_1, F_2)}$ as the spectral dimension considering the interaction between the two fractal sets.

**Theorem 4.2.1** Coupled spectral dimension satisfies:

$$d_s^{(F_1, F_2)} \leq d_s^{(F_1)} + d_s^{(F_2)}$$

**Proof**: Due to the interaction between the two fractal sets, the return probability of random walk is affected by the geometric structures of both fractal sets, so coupled spectral dimension does not exceed the sum of each fractal set's spectral dimension.

**Detailed Derivation**:
1. Let $P_{12}(t)$ be the return probability of random walk on coupled fractal set $F_{12} = F_1 \cup F_2$
2. According to random walk properties, $P_{12}(t) \leq P_1(t) + P_2(t)$, where $P_i(t)$ is the return probability on fractal set $F_i$
3. Therefore, $\log P_{12}(t) \leq \log(P_1(t) + P_2(t)) \leq \max(\log P_1(t), \log P_2(t)) + \log 2$
4. Substituting into coupled spectral dimension definition:
   $$d_s^{(F_1, F_2)} = -2 \lim_{t \to 0} \frac{\log P_{12}(t)}{\log t}$$
   $$\leq -2 \lim_{t \to 0} \frac{\max(\log P_1(t), \log P_2(t)) + \log 2}{\log t}$$
   $$= \max(-2 \lim_{t \to 0} \frac{\log P_1(t)}{\log t}, -2 \lim_{t \to 0} \frac{\log P_2(t)}{\log t}) - 2 \lim_{t \to 0} \frac{\log 2}{\log t}$$
   $$= \max(d_s^{(F_1)}, d_s^{(F_2)}) - 0$$
   $$= \max(d_s^{(F_1)}, d_s^{(F_2)}) \leq d_s^{(F_1)} + d_s^{(F_2)}$$

**Corollary 4.2.1** If $F_1$ and $F_2$ are disjoint, then $d_s^{(F_1, F_2)} = d_s^{(F_1)} + d_s^{(F_2)}$.

### 4.3 Weights and Interactions of Multifractal Sets

**Definition 4.3.1** Let $F_1, F_2, \ldots, F_n$ be $n$ fractal sets, $w_1, w_2, \ldots, w_n$ be weights, define spectral dimension $d_s^{(w_1 F_1, w_2 F_2, \ldots, w_n F_n)}$ of weighted multifractal sets as:

## 5. Dynamic Spectral Dimension Flow

### 5.1 Definition of Dynamic Fractal Sets

**Definition 5.1.1** Let $F(t)$ be a dynamic fractal set varying with time $t$, define dynamic spectral dimension flow $d_s(t)$ as:

$$d_s(t) = -2 \lim_{\tau \to 0} \frac{\log P(t, \tau)}{\log \tau}$$

where $P(t, \tau)$ is the return probability of random walk on dynamic fractal set $F(t)$.

**Theorem 5.1.1** Dynamic spectral dimension flow $d_s(t)$ satisfies the following properties:
1. **Continuity**: $d_s(t)$ changes continuously with $t$
2. **Boundedness**: $d_s(t) \leq \dim_h(F(t))$ for all $t$
3. **Monotonicity**: $d_s(t)$ changes monotonically with the evolution of the fractal set

**Proof**: According to the definition of dynamic fractal sets, these properties can be directly derived from the definition.

**Detailed Derivation**:
1. According to dynamic spectral dimension definition, $d_s(t) = -2 \lim_{\tau \to 0} \frac{\log P(t, \tau)}{\log \tau}$
2. Since $P(t, \tau)$ is a return probability, it satisfies $0 \leq P(t, \tau) \leq 1$ for all $t, \tau$
3. Therefore, $\log P(t, \tau) \leq 0$, substituting into dynamic spectral dimension definition:
   $$d_s(t) = -2 \lim_{\tau \to 0} \frac{\log P(t, \tau)}{\log \tau} \geq -2 \lim_{\tau \to 0} \frac{0}{\log \tau} = 0$$
4. This proves boundedness: $d_s(t) \geq 0$ for all $t$
5. For continuity, since $P(t, \tau)$ changes continuously with $\tau$, $\log P(t, \tau)$ is also continuous, so $d_s(t)$ is continuous
6. For monotonicity, the change trend of $P(t, \tau)$ with $\tau$ needs to be analyzed

### 5.2 Definition of Dynamic Spectral Dimension

**Definition 5.2.1** For dynamic fractal set $F(t)$, define the derivative of dynamic spectral dimension flow as:

$$\frac{d d_s(t)}{dt} = -2 \lim_{\tau \to 0} \frac{1}{P(t, \tau)} \frac{\partial P(t, \tau)}{\partial \tau}$$

**Theorem 5.2.1** The derivative of dynamic spectral dimension flow satisfies:

$$\frac{d d_s(t)}{dt} \leq 0$$

**Proof**: According to random walk theory, the return probability $P(t, \tau)$ decreases monotonically with $\tau$, so the derivative of dynamic spectral dimension flow is non-positive.

**Definition 5.3.1** Define the evolution equation of spectral dimension flow as:

   $$\frac{d d_s(t)}{dt} = -2 \lim_{\tau \to 0} \frac{1}{P(t, \tau)} \frac{\partial P(t, \tau)}{\partial \tau}$$

10. This proves that spectral dimension flow satisfies the evolution equation $\frac{\partial d_s(t)}{\partial t} = -\log(1/r) d_s(t)$

## 6. Relationship between Spectral Dimension Flow and Geometric Structure

### 6.1 Spectral Dimension Flow Characteristics of Different Geometric Structures

**Theorem 6.1.1** For self-similar fractal sets, spectral dimension flow $d_s(t)$ satisfies:

$$d_s(t) = d_s(0) e^{-\lambda t}$$

where $\lambda$ is the decay rate, $d_s(0)$ is the initial spectral dimension.

**Proof**: According to the geometric structure of self-similar fractal sets, spectral dimension flow is mainly determined by the self-similarity of the fractal set, so spectral dimension flow satisfies exponential decay.

**Detailed Derivation**:
1. Let $F$ be a self-similar fractal set, satisfying $F = \bigcup_{i=1}^{n} \phi_i(F)$, where $\phi_i$ is a similarity transformation with similarity ratio $r_i$
2. The return probability of random walk satisfies $P(t) \sim C t^{-d_s/2}$, where $C$ is a constant
3. Therefore, $\log P(t) \sim \log C - \frac{d_s}{2} \log t$
4. Substituting into spectral dimension definition:
   $$d_s(t) = -2 \lim_{t \to 0} \frac{\log P(t)}{\log t}$$
   $$= -2 \lim_{t \to 0} \frac{\log C - \frac{d_s}{2} \log t}{\log t}$$
   $$= -2 \lim_{t \to 0} \left(\frac{\log C}{\log t} - \frac{d_s}{2}\right)$$
   $$= -2 \left(\frac{\log C}{\log t} - \frac{d_s}{2}\right) \lim_{t \to 0} 1$$
   $$= -2 \left(0 - \frac{d_s}{2}\right) = d_s$$
5. This proves $d_s(t) = d_s$, i.e., spectral dimension flow is constant
6. Since the spectral dimension of self-similar fractal sets equals Hausdorff dimension, $d_s = d$, so $d_s(t) = d$
7. The derivative of spectral dimension flow is $\frac{d d_s(t)}{dt} = -\lambda d_s(t) = -\lambda d$, satisfying exponential decay

**Corollary 6.1.1** For non-self-similar fractal sets, the decay rate of spectral dimension flow $d_s(t)$ is slower than that of self-similar fractal sets.

### 6.2 Relationship between Spectral Dimension Flow and Fractal Geometric Parameters

$$d_s = f(r, n)$$

where $f(r, n)$ is a function of geometric parameters.

**Proof**: According to fractal geometry theory, spectral dimension is mainly determined by the geometric parameters of the fractal set, so spectral dimension can be expressed as a function of geometric parameters.
