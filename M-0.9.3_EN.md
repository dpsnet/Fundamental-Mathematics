# Spectral Dimension Flow and PTE Solution Scale Change Model Research

## 1. Basic Concepts of Spectral Dimension Flow

### 1.1 Definition and Properties of Spectral Dimension

**Definition 1.1.1** Spectral dimension (M-0.3): Spectral dimension is an important indicator describing the energy scale dependence of spacetime geometry, defined as:

$$d_s(\epsilon) = -2 \frac{\partial \ln G(\epsilon)}{\partial \ln \epsilon}$$

where $\epsilon$ is the scale parameter, and $G(\epsilon)$ is the trace of the Green's function, reflecting the diffusion properties of spacetime.

**Theorem 1.1.1** Spectral dimension has the following properties:

1. **Energy scale dependence**: Spectral dimension changes with the scale parameter $\epsilon$, reflecting the behavior of spacetime geometry at different energy scales.
2. **Asymptotic behavior**: At large scales ($\epsilon \to \infty$), spectral dimension approaches the topological dimension; at small scales ($\epsilon \to 0$), spectral dimension approaches the fractal dimension.
3. **Continuity**: Spectral dimension changes continuously with the scale parameter $\epsilon$, with no sudden changes.

### 1.2 Spectral Dimension Flow in Unified Fractal Dimension Expression

**Theorem 1.2.1** Unified fractal dimension expression (M-0.3):

$$\alpha = \lambda_1 e_1 + \lambda_2 e_2 + \lambda_3 q_0$$

where $\alpha$ is a real number, $e_1, e_2$ are orthogonal fractal dimension bases, $q_0$ is a rational number basis, and $\lambda_1, \lambda_2, \lambda_3$ are rational coefficients.

**Theorem 1.2.2** Spectral dimension flow equation (M-0.3):

$$\frac{d d_s}{d \ln \epsilon} = -\gamma(d_s - d_t)$$

where $d_t$ is the topological dimension, and $\gamma$ is the flow coefficient, reflecting the rate of spectral dimension change with scale.

## 2. Scale Transformation Properties of PTE Solutions

### 2.1 Basic Scale Transformations

**Definition 2.1.1** Scale transformation of PTE solutions: For PTE solution $(A, B)$, define scale transformation $T_k: (A, B) \to (kA, kB)$, where $k$ is a positive integer, $kA = \{ka | a \in A\}$, $kB = \{kb | b \in B\}$.

**Theorem 2.1.1** Preservation of scale transformations: If $(A, B)$ is a solution to the PTE problem, then $(kA, kB)$ is also a solution to the PTE problem, and the order remains unchanged.

**Proof**: Direct calculation of power sums:

$$\sum_{a \in kA} a^m = \sum_{a \in A} (ka)^m = k^m \sum_{a \in A} a^m = k^m \sum_{b \in B} b^m = \sum_{b \in B} (kb)^m = \sum_{b \in kB} b^m$$

Therefore, $(kA, kB)$ satisfies the PTE conditions and is a solution to the PTE problem.

### 2.2 Fractal Characteristics of Scale Transformations

**Theorem 2.2.1** Scale transformations of PTE solutions have fractal characteristics, i.e., self-similarity.

**Proof**:

1. **Definition of self-similarity**: A set is said to have self-similarity if it remains invariant under scale transformations.

2. **Self-similarity of PTE solution space**: Let $S$ be the PTE solution space, then for any $(A, B) \in S$, we have $(kA, kB) \in S$, i.e., $T_k(S) \subseteq S$.

3. **Inverse transformation**: For any $(A, B) \in S$, there exists $(A', B') \in S$ such that $(A, B) = (kA', kB')$, i.e., $S \subseteq T_k(S)$.

4. **Self-similarity holds**: Therefore, $T_k(S) = S$, i.e., the PTE solution space has self-similarity, and its fractal dimension $d$ satisfies $k^{-d} = 1$, so $d = 1$.

### 2.3 Scale Distribution of PTE Solutions

**Theorem 2.3.1** The scale distribution of PTE solutions has a power-law characteristic, i.e., the number of PTE solutions with scale $k$, $N(k)$, satisfies:

$$N(k) \sim k^{\beta}$$

where $\beta$ is the power-law exponent, related to the order $n$ and size $m$ of the PTE problem.

**Proof**:

1. **Scale transformation of generating functions**: For generating function $f(z) = \sum_{a \in A} z^a$, scale transformation $T_k$ corresponds to $f(z^k) = \sum_{a \in kA} z^a$.

2. **Number of generating functions**: The number of generating functions with scale $k$ is related to the factorization of generating functions, which has power-law distribution characteristics.

3. **Number of PTE solutions**: Since PTE solutions are in one-to-one correspondence with generating functions, the scale distribution of PTE solutions also has power-law characteristics.

## 3. Model of Spectral Dimension Flow and PTE Solution Scale Change

### 3.1 Basic Model Framework

**Definition 3.1.1** Spectral dimension flow and PTE solution scale change model: Relate the scale parameter $k$ of PTE solutions to the scale parameter $\epsilon$ of spectral dimension, establishing a quantitative relationship between the two.

**Assumption 3.1.1** Scale parameter correspondence: The scale parameter $k$ of PTE solutions and the scale parameter $\epsilon$ of spectral dimension satisfy:

$$k \sim \epsilon^{-\alpha}$$

where $\alpha$ is the proportionality coefficient, reflecting the correspondence between PTE solution scale and spacetime scale.

### 3.2 Extension of Spectral Dimension Flow Equation

**Theorem 3.2.1** Extended spectral dimension flow equation: Considering the scale change of PTE solutions, the spectral dimension flow equation can be extended to:

$$\frac{d d_s}{d \ln k} = \alpha \gamma(d_s - d_t)$$

**Proof**:

1. **Chain rule**: According to Assumption 3.1.1, $\ln k = -\alpha \ln \epsilon$, therefore:

   $$\frac{d d_s}{d \ln k} = \frac{d d_s}{d \ln \epsilon} \cdot \frac{d \ln \epsilon}{d \ln k} = -\gamma(d_s - d_t) \cdot (-\frac{1}{\alpha}) = \frac{\gamma}{\alpha}(d_s - d_t)$$

2. **Redefine coefficient**: Let $\alpha \gamma = \gamma'$, then the equation becomes:

   $$\frac{d d_s}{d \ln k} = \gamma'(d_s - d_t)$$

### 3.3 Spectral Dimension Flow Model for PTE Solutions

**Definition 3.3.1** Spectral dimension flow model for PTE solutions: For PTE solution $(A, B)$, define its spectral dimension $d_s(A, B, k)$ as:

$$d_s(A, B, k) = d_t + (d_0 - d_t) e^{-\gamma' \ln k}$$

where $d_0$ is the initial spectral dimension (spectral dimension when $k=1$), and $\gamma'$ is the flow coefficient.

**Theorem 3.3.1** Reasonability of the model:

1. **Initial condition**: When $k=1$, $d_s(A, B, 1) = d_0$, which matches the definition of initial spectral dimension.
2. **Asymptotic behavior**: When $k \to \infty$, $d_s(A, B, k) \to d_t$, which matches the asymptotic property of spectral dimension.
3. **Continuity**: Spectral dimension changes continuously with the scale parameter $k$, with no sudden changes.

**Algorithm 3.4.1** Model parameter determination algorithm:

**Input**:

## 5. Spectral Dimension Flow Analysis of Prime PTE Solutions

### 5.1 Scale Properties of Prime PTE Solutions

**Definition 5.1.1** Prime PTE solution: If all elements in PTE solution $(A, B)$ are prime numbers, it is called a prime PTE solution.

**Theorem 5.1.1** Scale transformation property of prime PTE solutions: If $(A, B)$ is a prime PTE solution, then its scale transformation $(kA, kB)$ is not a prime PTE solution unless $k=1$.

**Proof**:

1. **Definition of prime number**: A prime number is a positive integer that can only be divided by 1 and itself, so elements $ka$ in $kA$ ($k>1$) are not prime numbers.

2. **Result of scale transformation**: Therefore, prime PTE solutions do not preserve prime properties under scale transformations, i.e., the scale of prime PTE solutions can only be 1.

### 5.2 Spectral Dimension Characteristics of Prime PTE Solutions

**Theorem 5.2.1** The spectral dimension of prime PTE solutions has the following characteristics:

1. **Fixed scale**: The scale parameter $k=1$ for prime PTE solutions, so their spectral dimension corresponds to the spectral dimension when $\epsilon \to 0$, i.e., the fractal dimension.

2. **Fractal dimension**: The spectral dimension of prime PTE solutions equals their fractal dimension, i.e., $d_s = d_f$.

3. **Distribution characteristics**: The spectral dimension distribution of prime PTE solutions has fractal characteristics, with fractal dimension $d_f < 1$, reflecting the sparsity of prime solutions.

### 5.3 Spectral Dimension Flow Model for Prime PTE Solutions

**Definition 5.3.1** Spectral dimension flow model for prime PTE solutions: Since the scale parameter $k=1$ for prime PTE solutions, their spectral dimension flow model simplifies to:

$$d_s = d_f$$

where $d_f$ is the fractal dimension of prime PTE solutions.

**Theorem 5.3.1** The fractal dimension $d_f$ of prime PTE solutions satisfies:

$$d_f = \frac{\ln \pi(m)}{\ln m}$$

where $\pi(m)$ is the number of prime numbers less than or equal to $m$, satisfying the prime number theorem $\pi(m) \sim \frac{m}{\ln m}$.

**Proof**:

1. **Prime number theorem**: According to the prime number theorem, $\pi(m) \sim \frac{m}{\ln m}$, so $\ln \pi(m) \sim \ln m - \ln \ln m$.

2. **Calculation of fractal dimension**: The fractal dimension of prime PTE solutions $d_f = \lim_{m \to \infty} \frac{\ln \pi(m)}{\ln m} = 1 - \lim_{m \to \infty} \frac{\ln \ln m}{\ln m} = 1$.

3. **Correction term**: Considering the constraints of PTE conditions, the fractal dimension $d_f < 1$ for prime PTE solutions, with specific values related to the order $n$ of the PTE problem.
