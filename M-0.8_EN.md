### 2.1 Existence Theorem of 4-dimensional Spacetime

**Theorem 2.1.1** (Existence of 4-dimensional Minkowski Spacetime): Based on M-0.1 fractal dimension theory, 4-dimensional Minkowski spacetime necessarily exists.

**Proof**:

**Step 1: Starting from Fractal Dimension Theory**

According to Theorem 1.1.1 of M-0.1, Hausdorff dimension $\dim_h(F)$ has the following basic properties:
1. **Monotonicity**: If $E \subseteq F$, then $\dim_h(E) \leq \dim_h(F)$
2. **Countable Stability**: $\dim_h\left(\bigcup_{i=1}^\infty F_i\right) = \sup_{i \geq 1} \dim_h(F_i)$
3. **Translation Invariance**: $\dim_h(F + x) = \dim_h(F)$

where the signature (1,3) indicates the time direction has positive sign, and space directions have negative signs.

**Step 4: Constructing 4-dimensional Minkowski Spacetime**

Define 4-dimensional Minkowski spacetime as the metric space $(M^4, d)$, where:
- $M^4 = \mathbb{R}^4$ is a 4-dimensional real vector space
- $d(x, y) = \sqrt{\langle x-y, x-y \rangle}$ is the Minkowski metric

Since $F_4$ is a subset of $\mathbb{R}^4$ with Hausdorff dimension 4, $(F_4, d|_{F_4})$ constitutes a 4-dimensional fractal spacetime.

**Conclusion**: 4-dimensional Minkowski spacetime necessarily exists.

**Step 4: Proving Necessity of Inner Product Signature**
