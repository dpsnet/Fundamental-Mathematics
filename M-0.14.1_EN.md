## 2. Mathematical Description of Higher-Dimensional Spectral Dimension Manifestation

### 2.1 Conceptual Clarification: Distinction Between Topological Dimension and Spectral Dimension

Before delving into extra dimensions, two core concepts must be strictly distinguished:

**Definition 2.1.0 (Distinction Between Topological Dimension and Spectral Dimension)**
- **Topological Dimension** (manifestation form): The geometric structure dimension of spacetime, fixed at 4 dimensions (3 space + 1 time), is the "hardware" structure of spacetime itself, unchanged with energy scale
- **Spectral Dimension** (energy manifestation): The effective dimensional characteristic of field propagation in spacetime, describing the number of dimensions "felt" by the field, a dynamic parameter varying with energy scale

**Key Distinction:**
- Topological dimension = Entity dimension of spacetime (fixed at 4 dimensions)
- Spectral dimension = Manifestation dimension of field propagation (varies with energy scale)
- $d_s > 4$ **does not indicate** the existence of additional topological dimensions, but rather indicates that field propagation exhibits higher-dimensional characteristics

### 2.2 Mathematical Description of Higher-Dimensional Spectral Dimension Manifestation

**Definition 2.2.1 (Higher-Dimensional Spectral Dimension Manifestation)** At the UV limit (high energy limit), the spectral dimension $d_{s,\text{UV}}$ can be greater than 4, indicating that field propagation exhibits additional "effective dimension" characteristics, rather than the existence of additional topological dimensions.

**Definition 2.2.2 (Effective Extra Dimension Number)** The effective extra dimension number is defined as:

$$n_{extra} = d_{s,\text{UV}} - 4$$

**Theorem 2.2.1 (Spectral Dimension-Manifestation Relation)** For field propagation with $n$ higher-dimensional spectral dimension manifestation characteristics, its spectral dimension satisfies:

$$d_s = 4 + n \cdot f(R_{eff}/l_s)$$

where $f$ is a function of effective compactification scale, $l_s$ is the detection length scale, and $n$ is the "equivalent dimension number" of higher-dimensional spectral dimension manifestation.

**Proof:**

1. In 4-dimensional spacetime, the spectral dimension $d_s = 4$ (by M-0.14), indicating that field propagation only "feels" the 4-dimensional macroscopic structure

2. When the detection scale $l > R_{eff}$ (effective decoherence scale), field propagation only "feels" the 4-dimensional macroscopic structure, $d_s = 4$

3. When the detection scale $l < R_{eff}$, field propagation begins to "feel" the fine levels of microscopic fractal structure, exhibiting $d_s > 4$

4. The transition function $f(x)$ satisfies: $f(x) \to 0$ as $x \to \infty$ (low energy, large detection scale), $f(x) \to 1$ as $x \to 0$ (high energy, small detection scale)

5. Therefore, the total spectral dimension is the weighted sum of 4-dimensional basic manifestation and $n$ higher-dimensional manifestation characteristics ∎

## 3. Decoherence Mechanism of Spectral Dimension Manifestation

### 3.1 Mathematical Definition of Decoherence

**Definition 3.1.1 (Spectral Dimension Decoherence)** Spectral dimension decoherence refers to the process in which higher-dimensional spectral dimension manifestation degenerates into 4-dimensional form as the detection scale increases. The traditional terminology refers to this as "compactification," but this module emphasizes: this is **decoherence of manifestation dimension**, rather than actual curling of topological dimensions.

**Definition 3.1.2 (Effective Compactification Scale)** The effective compactification scale $R_{eff}$ is the characteristic scale describing the spectral dimension decoherence process. When the detection scale $l > R_{eff}$, the higher-dimensional manifestation characteristics of field propagation decohere, retaining only 4-dimensional macroscopic manifestation.

### 3.2 Spectral Dimension Flow and Compactification Manifestation

**Theorem 3.2.1 (Spectral Dimension Flow Manifestation Theorem)** In the process of spectral dimension flowing from higher-dimensional manifestation to 4-dimensional manifestation, the "extra dimension" characteristics exhibited by field propagation gradually degenerate into compactified form.

**Proof:**

1. Consider the spectral dimension flow equation (from M-0.7):
   $$\frac{\partial d_s}{\partial \ln k} = -\gamma(d_s - 4)$$

2. The solution is: $d_s(k) = 4 + (d_{s,\text{UV}} - 4)(k/k_{\text{UV}})^{\gamma}$

3. Define effective manifestation dimension: $d_{eff}(k) = d_s(k)$ (Note: this is **manifestation** of field propagation, not topological dimension)

4. When $k$ decreases from $k_{\text{UV}}$ to $k_{\text{IR}}$:
   - At $k = k_{\text{UV}}$: $d_{eff} = d_{s,\text{UV}}$ (field propagation exhibits higher-dimensional characteristics)
   - At $k = k_{\text{IR}}$: $d_{eff} = 4$ (field propagation degenerates into 4-dimensional manifestation)

5. Physical interpretation: When $d_s > 4$, field propagation "detects" fine levels of fractal structure at microscopic scales, exhibiting higher-dimensional propagation; as $d_s \to 4$, only the macroscopic 4-dimensional structure can be detected. The topological dimension always remains fixed at 4.

6. Compactification interpretation: The "compactification" of "extra dimensions" is **decoherence of manifestation dimension** in spectral dimension flow, rather than actual curling of topological dimensions ∎

### 3.3 Formula for Effective Compactification Scale

**Theorem 3.3.1 (Effective Compactification Scale Formula)** The effective compactification scale describing spectral dimension decoherence is given by:

$$R_{compact}(E) = l_P \cdot \left(\frac{E}{E_P}\right)^{-\alpha}$$

where:
- $l_P$ is the Planck length
- $E_P$ is the Planck energy
- $\alpha = (d_{s,\text{UV}} - 4)/2$ is the critical exponent

**Proof:**

1. By dimensional analysis, the compactification scale should have the dimension of length

2. In natural units, the only fundamental length scale is the Planck length $l_P = 1/E_P$

3. The energy dependence is given by renormalization group analysis

4. The exponent $\alpha$ is determined by the scaling property of spectral dimension flow ∎

**Corollary 3.3.1:** At the Planck energy scale $E = E_P$, $R_{eff} = l_P$, and higher-dimensional spectral dimension manifestation is fully expanded; at low energy $E \ll E_P$, $R_{eff} \gg l_P$ (relative detection scale), and higher-dimensional spectral dimension manifestation decoheres into 4-dimensional form.

**Physical Interpretation:**
- "Effective compactification" describes the decoherence of field propagation sensitivity to microscopic fractal structures
- At high energies ($E \sim E_P$), the detection wavelength $\sim l_P$, and field propagation can "feel" fine levels of microscopic fractal structures, exhibiting $d_s > 4$
- At low energies ($E \ll E_P$), the detection wavelength $\gg l_P$, and field propagation can only "feel" the macroscopic 4-dimensional structure, $d_s \to 4$
