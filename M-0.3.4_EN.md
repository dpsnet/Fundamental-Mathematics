### 2.2 Basic Concepts of Quantization

Quantization is the process of converting classical physical quantities into quantum physical quantities, mainly including the following two methods [5]:

1. **Canonical Quantization**: Convert canonical variables in classical mechanics (such as position and momentum) into operators in quantum mechanics, satisfying the commutation relation:
   $$[\hat{x}, \hat{p}] = i\hbar$$

2. **Path Integral Quantization**: Calculate the transition amplitude of quantum states through integration over all possible paths:
   $$\langle x_f, t_f | x_i, t_i \rangle = \int_{x(t_i)=x_i}^{x(t_f)=x_f} \mathcal{D}x(t) e^{iS[x(t)]/\hbar}$$

In this paper, we will adopt the canonical quantization method to convert the rational coefficients and rational terms in the unified fractal dimension expression into quantum operators.

### 2.3 Mathematical Properties of Rational Numbers

Rational numbers are numbers that can be expressed as the ratio of two integers, i.e., $r = p/q$, where $p, q \in \mathbb{Z}$ and $q \neq 0$. Rational numbers have the following important properties [6]:

1. **Countability**: The set of rational numbers $\mathbb{Q}$ is countable, and a one-to-one correspondence can be established with the set of natural numbers $\mathbb{N}$.

2. **Density**: The set of rational numbers $\mathbb{Q}$ is dense in the set of real numbers $\mathbb{R}$, that is, there are infinitely many rational numbers between any two real numbers.

3. **Closure**: The set of rational numbers $\mathbb{Q}$ is closed under addition, subtraction, multiplication, and division (except division by zero), forming a field.

4. **Separability**: The set of rational numbers $\mathbb{Q}$ can be expressed as a linear combination of integers, that is, any rational number $r$ can be expressed as $r = \sum_i n_i \cdot r_i$, where $n_i \in \mathbb{Z}$, $r_i \in \mathbb{Q}$.

These properties provide a mathematical foundation for the quantization of rational numbers, especially countability and closure, which allow rational numbers to be used as values of quantum numbers.
