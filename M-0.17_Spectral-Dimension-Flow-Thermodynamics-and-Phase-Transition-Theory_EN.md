## 1. Zeroth Law of Spectral Dimension Thermodynamics

### 1.1 Problem Statement

The zeroth law of classical thermodynamics allows us to define temperature as an indicator of thermal equilibrium. However, when systems are at different effective dimensions:
- System A: $d_s^{(A)} = 4$ (classical spacetime)
- System B: $d_s^{(B)} = 2$ (fractal core)

Does the traditional temperature definition still apply? Can systems of different dimensions reach thermal equilibrium?

### 1.2 Spectral Dimension Entropy and Spectral Dimension Temperature

**Definition (Spectral Dimension Entropy)**: In effective spacetime of $d_s$ dimensions, entropy is a function of energy and dimension:
$$S(E, d_s) = k_B \left[\alpha(d_s) \ln\frac{E}{E_0} + \frac{d_s}{3}\ln\frac{V}{V_0}\right]$$

where $\alpha(d_s)$ is the energy exponent dependent on spectral dimension.

**Definition (Spectral Dimension Temperature)**:
$$\boxed{\frac{1}{T(d_s)} = \left(\frac{\partial S}{\partial E}\right)_{V, d_s} = \frac{k_B \alpha(d_s)}{E}}$$

That is:
$$T(d_s) = \frac{E}{k_B \alpha(d_s)}$$

**Key Insight**: Systems with the same energy $E$ have **different temperatures** at different effective dimensions!

### 1.3 Complete Statement of Zeroth Law of Spectral Dimension

> **Theorem 1.1 (Zeroth Law of Spectral Dimension)**: Two systems reach thermal equilibrium if and only if:
> 1. **Isothermal Condition**: $T_A = T_B$ (spectral dimension temperatures are equal)
> 2. **Isodimensional Condition**: $\mu_d^{(A)} = \mu_d^{(B)}$ (spectral dimension chemical potentials are equal)
>
> Where spectral dimension chemical potential is defined as:
> $$\mu_d = -T\left(\frac{\partial S}{\partial d_s}\right)_{E,V} = -k_B T \left[\alpha'(d_s)\ln\frac{E}{E_0} + \frac{1}{3}\ln\frac{V}{V_0}\right]$$

### 1.4 Spectral Dimension Explanation of Black Hole Thermal Equilibrium

Consider the black hole-heat reservoir system:
- Heat reservoir ($d_s = 4$): $T_{\text{reservoir}} = T_0$
- Horizon ($d_s = 3$): $T_{\text{horizon}} = T_0 \cdot \frac{\alpha(4)}{\alpha(3)}$
- Interior ($d_s = 2$): $T_{\text{interior}} = T_0 \cdot \frac{\alpha(4)}{\alpha(2)}$

**Conclusion**: The "temperature" measured by observers inside and outside the black hole are different, but this is a normal phenomenon dependent on spectral dimension, not thermal imbalance!

## 4. Inter-dimensional Transport Phenomena

### 4.1 Inter-dimensional Heat Conduction Equation

Heat flow equation:
$$\mathbf{J}_Q = -\kappa_T \nabla T - \kappa_d \nabla d_s$$

$$\mathbf{J}_Q^{(d)} = -\kappa_d \nabla d_s$$

When $\nabla T \neq 0$ but $\mathbf{J}_Q = 0$ (open circuit):
$$\nabla\frac{\mu_d}{T} = -\frac{L_{Qd}}{L_{dd}} \nabla\frac{1}{T}$$

**Physical Significance**: Temperature gradients produce spectral dimension chemical potential gradients.

### 4.3 Inter-dimensional Peltier Effect

When $\nabla T = 0$ but $\mathbf{J}_d \neq 0$:
$$\mathbf{J}_Q = -\frac{L_{Qd}}{L_{dd}} \mathbf{J}_d$$

**Physical Significance**: Inter-dimensional flow carries heat!

3. Iso-dimensional cooling ($d_s = d_2$): Release heat $Q_c$
4. Adiabatic dimensional increase ($d_s: d_2 \to d_1$): Consume work
