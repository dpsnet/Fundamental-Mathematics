This paper establishes a complete quantum statistical mechanics framework under spectral dimension flow, covering non-relativistic and relativistic cases, equilibrium and non-equilibrium states. The theory also generalizes Jarzynski fluctuation theorem and thermodynamic uncertainty relations to fractal systems, providing new statistical mechanical foundations for resolving the black hole information paradox.

## 1. Foundations of Spectral Dimension Quantum Statistics

### 1.1 Density of States in Fractal Spacetime

In effective spacetime of $d_s$ dimensions, the density of states for free particles:

**Non-relativistic case**:
$$\boxed{g_{\text{nr}}(E, d_s) = A(d_s) \cdot V \cdot E^{d_s/2 - 1}}$$

**Relativistic case**:
$$\boxed{g_{\text{rel}}(E, d_s) = B(d_s) \cdot V \cdot E^{d_s - 1}}$$

where coefficients:
$$A(d_s) = \frac{(2m)^{d_s/2}}{2^{d_s-1}\pi^{d_s/2}\Gamma(d_s/2)\hbar^{d_s}}$$
$$B(d_s) = \frac{2\pi^{d_s/2}}{(2\pi)^{d_s}\Gamma(d_s/2)(\hbar c)^{d_s}}$$

### 1.2 Grand Partition Function

$$\ln \mathcal{Z} = \pm \frac{V}{(2\pi)^{d_s}} \int d^{d_s}k \, \ln(1 \pm z e^{-\beta\varepsilon_k})$$

where $z = e^{\beta\mu}$ is fugacity, $\pm$ corresponds to fermions/bosons respectively.

## 2. Bose-Einstein Statistics

### 2.1 Spectral Dimension Bose Distribution

Distribution function form remains unchanged:
$$\bar{n}_{BE}(E) = \frac{1}{e^{\beta(E-\mu)} - 1}$$

But density of states changes lead to changes in macroscopic properties.

### 2.2 Dimensional Conditions for BEC

Critical temperature:
$$\boxed{T_c(d_s) = \left[\frac{n}{A(d_s)\Gamma(d_s/2)\zeta(d_s/2)}\right]^{2/d_s}}$$

**Theorem 2.1 (BEC Dimensional Theorem)**:
- $d_s > 2$: Strict BEC exists
- $d_s = 2$: Kosterlitz-Thouless phase transition (quasi-condensation)
- $d_s < 2$: No condensation

### 2.3 Equation of State

$$P V = \frac{2}{d_s} U$$

- $d_s = 3$: $PV = \frac{2}{3}U$ (classical)
- $d_s = 2$: $PV = U$ (two-dimensional system)

## 3. Fermi-Dirac Statistics

### 3.1 Spectral Dimension Dependence of Fermi Energy

**Non-relativistic**:
$$E_F(d_s) = \left[\frac{d_s n}{2A(d_s)}\right]^{2/d_s}$$

**Relativistic**:
$$E_F(d_s) = \hbar c \left[\frac{(2\pi)^{d_s} d_s n}{2V_{d_s}}\right]^{1/d_s}$$

### 3.2 Heat Capacity

$$C_V \propto T \cdot T_F^{d_s/2 - 1}$$

- $d_s = 3$: $C_V \propto T$ (Fermi liquid)
- $d_s = 2$: $C_V \propto T$ (logarithmic correction)
- $d_s = 1$: $C_V \propto T / T_F^{1/2}$

### 4.1 Spectral Dimension Law of Blackbody Radiation

Energy density:
$$\boxed{u = \sigma_{SB}(d_s) \cdot T^{d_s+1}}$$

$$\sigma_{SB}(d_s) = \frac{B(d_s) \Gamma(d_s) \zeta(d_s) d_s k_B^{d_s+1}}{(\hbar c)^{d_s}}$$

$$E_{\text{max}} = x(d_s) \cdot k_B T$$

$$\boxed{\langle e^{-\beta W - \gamma W_d} \rangle = e^{-\beta \Delta F - \gamma \Delta F_d}}$$

$$\frac{P_F(W, W_d)}{P_R(-W, -W_d)} = \exp\left[\beta(W - \Delta F) + \gamma(W_d - \Delta F_d)\right]$$
